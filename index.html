About SonRise News

Established 2025 — Nairobi & Beyond

SonRise News is an independent digital news platform committed to delivering fast, accurate, and impactful journalism across breaking news, sports, politics, entertainment, technology, and business.

We believe that informed communities are empowered communities. Every story we publish is driven by the same question: What does the next generation need to know?

Our Values

At SonRise News, we are guided by three core principles: Truth — we report facts and verify before we publish. Impact — every story we choose matters to the people we serve. Legacy — we build journalism that stands the test of time, story by story.

Our Editorial Standards

SonRise News holds itself to the highest standards of responsible journalism. We verify sources, correct errors transparently, and never allow commercial relationships to influence editorial decisions. When we get something wrong, we say so — clearly and promptly.

Why SonRise?

Just as the sun rises without fail every single morning, SonRise News commits to showing up for our readers every day — with stories that inform, perspectives that challenge, and coverage that empowers communities across Kenya and beyond.

What We Cover

  • Breaking News — Real-time coverage of events as they unfold
  • Sports — Local and international sports, scores, and analysis
  • Politics — Government, elections, policy, and accountability
  • Entertainment — Film, music, celebrity, culture, and lifestyle
  • Technology — Innovation, AI, startups, gadgets, and digital trends
  • Business — Markets, economy, entrepreneurship, and finance

Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how SonRise News uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website at sonrisenews.com (or agunextech.github.io). By using our site, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help the website remember your preferences, understand how you use the site, and serve relevant content and advertisements.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies — These are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core features such as page navigation and access to secure areas. The site cannot function correctly without these cookies.
  • Analytics Cookies — We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our site. These cookies collect information such as which pages are visited most, how long visitors stay, and how they arrived at the site. All data is anonymised and aggregated.
  • Advertising Cookies — SonRise News uses Google AdSense to display advertisements. Google and its advertising partners use cookies to serve ads that are relevant to your interests, based on your browsing activity across websites.
  • Preference Cookies — These cookies remember your preferences such as language settings, to provide a more personalised experience on return visits.

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our site are set by third-party services we use, including Google (Analytics and AdSense), social media platforms (when you use share buttons), and embedded media providers. These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies which we encourage you to review.

Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or receive a warning before a cookie is stored. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

To opt out of Google Analytics, install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. To manage Google advertising cookies, visit Google's Ads Settings.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Continued use of SonRise News constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

Editorial Standards

Our commitment to accuracy, fairness, and independence

SonRise News is committed to the highest standards of journalism. These Editorial Standards guide every story we publish, every source we quote, and every editorial decision we make. They are not just rules — they are our promise to our readers.

Accuracy

We verify all facts before publication. We consult multiple sources on significant claims. We do not publish information we cannot verify. When we make an error, we correct it promptly, transparently, and prominently.

Fairness and Balance

We present all sides of a story fairly. When reporting on disputes or controversies, we seek comment from all parties involved. We do not allow our coverage to be shaped by personal bias, political affiliation, or commercial interest.

Independence

SonRise News editorial decisions are made independently of advertisers, sponsors, and commercial partners. No story is published, altered, or suppressed in exchange for financial consideration. Sponsored content is clearly labelled as such at all times.

Source Protection

We protect the identity of confidential sources. When a source requests anonymity, we honour that request and do not publish information that could identify them without their consent.

Corrections Policy

We correct errors as soon as they are identified. Corrections are published on the original article, clearly marked, and dated. We do not silently alter or delete published articles without noting the change.

Discrimination and Hate

SonRise News does not publish content that discriminates against or incites hatred toward any individual or group based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other characteristic.

Conflicts of Interest

Our journalists and editors declare any potential conflicts of interest to editorial leadership before covering relevant stories. Where a conflict exists that cannot be managed, the journalist is removed from that story.

Contact the Editor

To raise a concern about our editorial standards or to file a complaint: [email protected]. We aim to respond to all editorial complaints within 5 working days.
